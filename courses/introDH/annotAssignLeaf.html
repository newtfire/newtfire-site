Your Annotation Assignment: Annotating to Re-mediate in Two Rounds

Re-mediation work literally involving multiple kinds of media with the original text medium of this narrative. It will also help us to connect the narrative of this story to forms of media that help us to understand and visualize its contexts that matter to us. Our work will literally be to illustrate the text of Leaf by Niggle with our annotations, though a full re-mediation of it might involve creating a virtual environment or an animation that helps people to visualize and experience the events and ideas of this story.

In your annotations, consider yourself to be doing two kinds of things:

  1. Explaining and discussing unfamiliar terms and concepts, and supplying more information that might help.
  2. Illustrating with images and media to enhance the reading experience as you wish.

Your annotations may be doing both things at once: explaining and illustrating because often a picture can explain or demonstrate something in a way that responds to the text you are reading. Try to provide artwork, illustrations, media that do the following:

  • Explore some of the symbolism or mythology in the story,
  • Help connect the story to historical context at the time Tolkien was writing this (in 1938 from England, the year before England became involved in World War II),
  • Help connect the story to contexts relevant to our time, perhaps in art or fiction or game communities, or elsewhere.
  • Might inspire or help lead to a re-creation of this narrative in some other artistic medium

We will do two rounds of annotations: the first round (in which each of you posts at least three annotations to begin), before our next class on Monday November 16. The next round will occur after we meet to discuss this complex story together, and you will add at least three more annotations to the first set, and possibly go back and modify annotations you first made if you want to add something to them.
